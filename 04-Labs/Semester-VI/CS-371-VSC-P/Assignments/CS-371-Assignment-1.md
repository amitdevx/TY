# CS-371 Assignment 1: Agile Fundamentals and Team Setup

## Problem Statement / Aim
To understand the fundamentals of Agile methodology, specifically Scrum, and to simulate the setup of a software development team with defined roles and ceremonies.

## Theory & Concept
Agile methodology is an iterative approach to software development and project management that helps teams deliver value to their customers faster and with fewer headaches. Scrum is a popular framework within Agile that emphasizes teamwork, accountability, and iterative progress toward a well-defined goal.

Key Scrum Roles:
1. **Product Owner:** Represents the business or customer community and is responsible for maximizing the value of the product. They manage the Product Backlog.
2. **Scrum Master:** A facilitator for an agile development team. They ensure the team follows scrum practices and clear obstacles.
3. **Development Team:** The professionals who do the work of delivering a potentially releasable increment of "Done" product at the end of each Sprint.

Scrum Ceremonies:
- Sprint Planning
- Daily Scrum
- Sprint Review
- Sprint Retrospective
