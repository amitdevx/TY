# Viva Questions: Knowledge Representation and Logical Reasoning

1. **What is Forward Chaining?**
   *Forward chaining is a data-driven reasoning strategy that starts with known facts, applies inference rules to generate new facts, and stops when a goal is reached or no more deductions can be made.*

2. **How does Forward Chaining differ from Backward Chaining?**
   *Forward chaining starts with data/facts and works towards a conclusion (data-driven), whereas backward chaining starts with a goal/conclusion and works backwards to see if the available facts support it (goal-driven).*

3. **What are expert systems?**
   *Expert systems are AI applications that emulate the decision-making ability of a human expert. They often rely heavily on rule-based logical reasoning, including forward and backward chaining.*

4. **What is propositional logic vs first-order logic?**
   *Propositional logic deals with simple declarative propositions that are true or false. First-order logic extends this by adding predicates, variables, and quantifiers (like "For all" or "There exists"), allowing for more complex representations of knowledge.*

5. **What is a Knowledge Base (KB)?**
   *A KB is a centralized repository for information used by AI systems. It typically contains a set of facts, definitions, and inference rules about a specific domain.*
